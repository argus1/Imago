The typical radiology image pipeline follows **a standardized progression from the moment a radiologic technologist captures a scan until a radiologist signs off on the final clinical report**. \[[1](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6371374/), [2](https://enlitic.com/blogs/radiology-workflow-efficiencies/), [3](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12127945/), [4](https://teleconsult.net/blog/radiology-workflow/#:~:text=In%20essence%2C%20a%20radiology%20workflow%20is%20the,analysing%20them%2C%20and%20finally%2C%20reporting%20the%20findings.), [5](https://blog.creliohealth.com/optimization-strategies-to-achieve-radiology-report-turnaround-time-standards/)\]

**1\. Image Acquisition & Metadata**

* **Capture:** The technologist captures the images (e.g., X-ray, CT, MRI, Ultrasound), converting energy into electrical and then raw digital signals.  
* **Metadata Logging:** Critical patient data (ID, modality, imaging parameters, and timestamp) is attached to the study. \[[2](https://enlitic.com/blogs/radiology-workflow-efficiencies/), [6](https://umsystem.pressbooks.pub/digitalradiographicexposure/chapter/image-capture-and-display/#:~:text=The%20basic%20process%20to%20get%20the%20radiographic,can%20be%20displayed%20on%20a%20video%20screen.), [7](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10487271/), [8](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-kZL4y5cuYY)\]

**2\. Pre-Processing & Quality Control (QC)**

* **Image Processing:** Raw data undergoes computer-aided adjustments like edge enhancement, contrast balancing, and noise reduction.  
* **Quality Control:** Technologists verify images to ensure proper positioning and exposure before sending the scan downstream. \[[3](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12127945/), [8](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-kZL4y5cuYY), [9](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pls-g8fSoJk), [10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7Sr5AJjlC4)\]

**3\. Routing & AI Triage**

* **PACS / VNA:** Images are formatted into the global DICOM standard and routed to a Picture Archiving and Communication System (PACS) or Vendor Neutral Archive (VNA).  
* **AI Integration:** Many modern pipelines automatically route images through AI algorithms prior to human viewing to detect anomalies or triage urgent cases. \[[2](https://enlitic.com/blogs/radiology-workflow-efficiencies/), [11](https://capestart.com/technology-blog/ai-in-medical-imaging/), [15](https://hellopearl.com/blog/ai-dental-radiograph-analysis-image-interpretation-pearl#:~:text=Most%20modern%20AI%20platforms%20connect%20directly%20with,in%20the%20same%20viewer%20you%20already%20use.), [16](https://www.ramsoft.com/blog/radiology-automation#:~:text=AI%2Denabled%20triage%20platforms%20analyze%20incoming%20imaging%20studies,pushed%20to%20the%20top%20of%20radiologists'%20worklists.), [17](https://blog.medicai.io/en/ai-imaging-cloud-pacs/#:~:text=AI%20models%20trained%20on%20large%20imaging%20datasets,receive%20immediate%20attention%20through%20AI%20healthcare%20solutions.)\]

**4\. Interpretation & Reporting**

* **Reading:** A radiologist accesses the  
  DICOM  
  study via a specialized diagnostic workstation to review the images.  
* **Reporting:** The radiologist dictates or writes a report containing clinical findings, impressions, and recommendations, which is then finalized. \[[1](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6371374/), [11](https://capestart.com/technology-blog/ai-in-medical-imaging/), [18](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3900223/)\]

**5\. Distribution & Archival**

* **Distribution:** The finalized report and linked images are sent back to the Electronic Health Record (EHR) to be viewed by the referring physician.  
* **Long-Term Archive:** The data is stored in long-term PACS systems for future reference and comparison. \[[2](https://enlitic.com/blogs/radiology-workflow-efficiencies/), [7](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10487271/), [19](https://shawndt.com/digital-radiography/), [20](https://blog.medicai.io/en/teleradiology/#:~:text=Images%20are%20acquired%20at%20the%20imaging%20facility%2C,typically%20within%20a%20contractually%20agreed%20turnaround%20time.), [21](https://curogram.com/blog/what-is-pacs-radiology-system#:~:text=Similarly%2C%20when%20a%20radiologist%20finalizes%20a%20report,and%20also%20to%20the%20patient's%20broader%20EMR/EHR.)\]
